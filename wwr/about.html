History

Whitewater rafting has roots in necessity and exploration. Early indigenous communities and explorers relied on simple rafts to traverse dangerous rivers. In 1811, an ill-fated expedition attempted to navigate Wyoming's Snake River but lacked proper equipment. Over time, rafting evolved from survival to sport, fueled by adventurers seeking thrilling challenges.

By the 1940s, surplus military rubber rafts made rafting more accessible. The 1970s saw the rise of commercial tours, bringing safety improvements and professional guides. Today, rafting is a global pursuit, with rapids graded from gentle floats to extreme Class V challenges. Whether for adrenaline or scenery, rivers worldwide offer unforgettable experiences!
